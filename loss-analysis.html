THE HONEST VERDICT

At current pricing, WaterWorks is a volunteer organization that accidentally charges money. With realistic time ranges, it's worse than the single-point estimates suggested:

  • Lawn mowing at $10 — $1-2/hr effective. On a big yard that takes an hour, you net one dollar. A vending machine has better margins.
  • Pressure washing at $30 — $7.50-10/hr. If a job runs long (stained concrete, large area), you're at $7.50/hr before taxes. That's a parking lot attendant who also has to bring their own equipment.
  • Car wash at $10 — $5.40-9/hr. A muddy SUV on a bad day puts you at $5.40/hr. The kid at the automated car wash pressing the "start" button makes more.
  • Snow driveway at $15 — $6.60-16.50/hr. This is your widest swing. A light dusting is almost decent money. A heavy storm is $6.60/hr of backbreaking labor in freezing weather.
  • $50k/year requires 22 jobs/day. On slow-job days, that's 22.8 hours of work — more hours than exist in a day, even before you factor in driving between jobs, eating, or sleeping.

The core problem: Your margin per job is thin ($1-15), and time is the hidden killer. A job that takes twice as long doesn't cost twice as much in supplies — but it cuts your hourly rate in half. You have zero buffer for slow days.
